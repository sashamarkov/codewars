If you feel difficult, please complete the <a href="http://www.codewars.com/kata/570ef7a834e61306da00035b" data-turbolinks="false" target="_blank">simple version</a></p><h2 id="task">Task</h2><p>Maybe you know such a game (or a magic), there are three hats (or cups, or other containers) on the table, one of them put in a thing(A ball, or a coin, or other small things), at this time quickly moving three hats(exchange their position), After stop moving, Let the player guess which hat has something in it.</p><p>Let's us play the game in this kata. </p><p>Suppose we have three hats on the table, Their positions are 1, 2 and 3. Put a thing in the middle of the hat(position 2), and then begin moving...</p><p>Give you a string array <code>exchange</code>, Array elements are similar to "1-2"(it means 1 and 2 exchange position),"3-1"(1 and 3 exchange position)...</p><p>Finally, please return the position of the hat (which one hidden something), <code>"1", "2", or "3"</code>(String form)</p><h2 id="examples">Examples</h2><pre><code>example1:exchange=["1-2","1-3","2-3"]at the beginning, put a ball into a hat at position 2"1-2"==&gt; exchange 1 and 2, then the ball move to position 1"1-3"==&gt; exchange 1 and 3, then the ball move to position 3"2-3"==&gt; exchange 2 and 3, then the ball move to position 2the exchange ends, and finally, the ball at position 2.So, sc(["1-2","1-3","2-3"]) should return "2"example2:exchange=["1-2","2-3","1-3"]at the beginning, put a ball into a hat at position 2"1-2"==&gt; exchange 1 and 2, then the ball move to position 1"2-3"==&gt; exchange 2 and 3, then the ball still at position 1"1-3"==&gt; exchange 1 and 3, then the ball move to position 3the exchange ends, and finally, the ball at position 3.So, sc(["1-2","1-3","2-3"]) should return "3"example3:exchange=["1-2","1-3","2-3","2-1","3-1","3-2"]ball moving: 2--&gt;1--&gt;3--&gt;2--&gt;1--&gt;3--2finally, the ball at position 2.sc(["1-2","1-3","2-3","1-2","1-3","2-3"]) should return "2"</code></pre><h2 id="code-length-calculation">Code length calculation</h2><p>In javascript, we can't get the user's real code, we can only get the system compiled code.

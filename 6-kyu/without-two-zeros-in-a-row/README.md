<h2><a href=https://www.codewars.com/kata/65cf8417e2b49c2ecd3c3aee/train/javascript target="_blank">Without two zeros in a row</a></h2><h3>6 kyu</h3><p>You are given a positive integer <em>n</em>.</p><p>The task is to calculate how many binary numbers without leading zeros (such that their length is <em>n</em> and they do <em>not</em> contain two zeros in a row) there are. Note that zero itself ("0") <em>meets</em> the conditions (for n = 1). </p><p>For example, there are three eligible binary numbers of length 3:</p><ol><li><em>101</em></li><li><em>110</em></li><li><em>111</em></li></ol><p><em>100 is not suitable.</em></p><p>Remember that <em>n</em> may be fairly big (up to 10^4). Good luck! </p>

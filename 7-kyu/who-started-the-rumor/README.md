<h2><a href=https://www.codewars.com/kata/69e8cd7f48dd4ffb1d25ef2f/train/python target="_blank">Who Started the Rumor?</a></h2><h3>7 kyu</h3><h3 id="overview">Overview</h3><p>Recently, there has been a rumor spreading, and you want to find who started it.</p><p>You begin questioning people involved in the rumor.Unfortunately, nobody is willing to tell you who they got the rumor from. However, they won't hesitate to tell you <strong>who they passed the rumor to</strong>.</p><p>This information is stored as a <code>record</code> (hashmap), where each person (string) is mapped to the people they told the rumor to (list of strings).</p><h3 id="task">Task</h3><p>Given the input <code>record</code>, determine who started the rumor using only the available information.</p><p>Everyone involved in the rumor is guaranteed to appear in the <code>record</code>, either as a key or within a value list.</p><p>Return the result as a list.</p><h3 id="note">Note</h3><ul><li>It is guaranteed to have at least 1 culprit</li><li>If there are multiple culprits, they must be sorted alphabetically.</li></ul><h3 id="example">Example</h3><pre><code class="language-text">1. record = {"Sarah": ["Elle"], "Larry":["Sarah","Elle"], "John":["Larry","May"],             "May":["Sarah"], "Elle":["May"]}  Output: ["John"]2. record = {"Mike":["Paige","Rose"], "Paige":["Hugh"], "Liam":["Rose"],             "Rose":["Paige", "Hugh"], "Hugh":["Liam"], "Victor":["Rose","Liam"]}  Output: ["Mike","Victor"]</code></pre>

<h2><a href=https://www.codewars.com/kata/686fd320adc800ab627089c2/train/javascript target="_blank">Shortest direction on circle</a></h2><h3>7 kyu</h3><p>Given coordinates of two points A and B on a circle determine what is the shortest direction from A to B.</p><p>To be clear with context, let's make some definitions:</p><ul><li><em>Direction</em> on a circle is number of degrees to advance along the circle from a point counterclockwise (positive sign) or clockwise (negative sign).</li><li><em>Length</em> of a direction is its absolute value.</li><li>A point <em>coordinate</em> is value of a direction to the point from the rightmost circle point.</li><li>As any number of full turns doesn't change actual point position, with this definition multiple coordinates may correspond to a same point.</li></ul><p>Conditions:</p><ol><li>coordinates are integer</li><li>coordinates are NOT limited to 0..360 and may be negative</li></ol><p>Examples:</p><ol><li>With A at 0 (right) and B at 90 (top) the shortest direction is counterclockwise +90.</li><li>If points are swapped in the previous example, the shortest direction is flipped to -90.</li><li>With A at 180 (left) and B at -450 (bottom) the shortest direction is counterclockwise +90.</li><li>With A at 90 (top) and B at 270 (bottom) both 180 and -180 are correct.</li></ol>

<h2><a href=https://www.codewars.com/kata/686e88bcedcbf29af056f499/train/sql target="_blank">SQL for Marketing  – Episode 1: Measuring Engagement Fatigue</a></h2><h3>5 kyu</h3><p>You’ve joined the data team at a multi-channel marketing platform handling millions of user engagement events — clicks, opens, replies — across email, SMS, and push notifications.</p><p>In an effort to improve conversion, the marketing team recently introduced more frequent campaign reminders — re-sending messages to users who haven’t responded within hours or days.</p><p>While this strategy increases message volume, it raises an important question:</p><p>“Does repeated messaging over a short period improve engagement, or does it lead to user fatigue?”</p><p>To answer this, you’ve been asked to model send fatigue — measuring how a user’s likelihood to engage changes depending on how many times they’ve been contacted in the past 7 days.</p><p>We have <code>campaign_sends</code> table: </p><ul><li><code>id</code> (int): primary key</li><li><code>user_id</code> (int): recipient</li><li><code>sent_at</code>    (timestamp): when the message left </li><li><code>campaign_id</code>    (varchar):    Marketing campaign identifier</li><li><code>channel</code>    (varchar):    'email', 'sms', or 'push'</li></ul><p>and <code>engagement_events</code> table:</p><ul><li><code>id</code> (int): primary key</li><li><code>user_id</code> (int): Unique user ID</li><li><code>event_time</code> (timestamp): when the engagement happened</li><li><code>campaign_id</code>    (varchar):    marketing campaign identifier</li><li><code>channel</code>    (varchar):    'email', 'sms', or 'push'</li><li><code>event_type</code> (varchar):    'click', 'open', 'reply', etc.</li></ul><p>You are tasked with analyzing send fatigue — the phenomenon where users may engage less as they receive more messages in a short time span.</p><p>For each message in the <code>campaign_sends</code> table:</p><ol><li><p>Compute how many previous messages the same user received in the 7 days before that message was sent: (<i>"How many times was this user messaged in the 7 days before this message?"</i>).</p><ul><li>This count is called <code>prior_touches</code></li><li>Only count messages from the same user</li><li>Only count messages strictly before the current send’s sent_at timestamp</li></ul></li><li><p>Check whether the user engaged with the campaign within 24 hours after the send time: (<i>"Did this message lead to an engagement in the 24 hours after it was sent?"</i>)</p><ul><li>This is your engagement flag (true/false)</li><li>Any matching engagement counts, regardless of channel or event type</li></ul></li><li><p>Group the results by <code>prior_touches</code>, and return final result that contains the following columns:</p><ol><li><code>prior_touches</code>    (integer):    Number of prior messages sent to the same user in the 7-day period before each send. </li><li><code>sends</code>    (integer):    Count of messages sent that had this exact prior_touches value. In other words, how many messages were sent to users who had already received this many prior messages in the past 7 days.</li><li><code>engagements</code> (integer): Number of those sends (from the sends column) that resulted in at least one engagement within 24 hours after send time, for the same user and campaign.</li><li><code>engagement_rate_pct</code> (varchar):    Percentage of engaged sends = 100 × engagements / sends, rounded to 2 decimal places.</li></ol></li></ol><p>Sort final result by <code>prior_touches</code> ascending.</p><h3 id="notes">Notes:</h3><ul><li><p>Touch frequency scope (for prior_touches):</p><ul><li>Count all prior sends for the same user in the last 7 days, regardless of campaign or channel.</li><li>In other words, fatigue is per user, not per campaign.</li></ul></li><li><p>Engagement scope (for success flag):</p><ul><li>An engagement matches only if it’s the same user and the same campaign within 24 hours after the send.</li><li>Channel doesn’t have to match.</li></ul></li><li><p>A message counts as a prior touch if it was sent in the 7-day period before the current send, including exactly 7 days ago, but not the current send itself.</p><ul><li>exactly 7 days ago --&gt; counts</li><li>7 days + 1 minute after --&gt; too late, does not count as prior</li><li>same moment as current send --&gt; does not count</li></ul></li><li><p>A message counts as engaged if the user interacted at or after the send time, but strictly before 24 hours have passed</p><ul><li>engagement time exactly equal to <code>sent_at</code> --&gt; included</li><li>engagement time is sent_at + 23h 59m 59s --&gt; included</li><li>engagement time is sent_at + 24h --&gt; excluded</li></ul></li><li><p>Duplicate-timestamp sends (same user, campaign, sent_at) are treated as separate messages; each is evaluated independently for prior-touch counting and can be flagged engaged by the same interaction event.</p></li><li><p>Users with no engagement events are still included in the output; their sends contribute to the sends count while their engagements value remains 0, producing an engagement rate of 0 % for those buckets.</p></li></ul><p>For this sample data:</p><pre><code>campaign_sends:| user_id | sent_at             | campaign_id | channel |+---------+---------------------+-------------+---------+|   1     | 2023-01-01 08:00:00 | winter      | email   ||   1     | 2023-01-03 09:00:00 | winter      | sms     ||   1     | 2023-01-05 10:00:00 | winter      | push    ||   2     | 2023-01-01 09:00:00 | winter      | email   ||   2     | 2023-01-02 10:00:00 | winter      | sms     ||   2     | 2023-01-04 11:00:00 | winter      | push    |</code></pre><pre><code>engagement_events: | user_id | event_time          | campaign_id | channel | event_type  |+---------+---------------------+-------------+---------+-------------+|   1     | 2023-01-01 08:30:00 | winter      | email   | click       ||   1     | 2023-01-03 12:00:00 | winter      | sms     | open        ||   2     | 2023-01-01 10:00:00 | winter      | email   | click       |</code></pre><p>Expected output is the following: </p><pre><code>| prior_touches  | sends  | engagements  | engagement_rate_pct  |+----------------+--------+--------------+----------------------+|       0        |   2    |      2       |       100.00         ||       1        |   2    |      1       |        50.00         ||       2        |   2    |      0       |         0.00         |</code></pre><p>User 1:</p><ul><li>On January 1st at 08:00, they received their first message of the week. Since they hadn't received any other messages in the prior 7 days, their prior_touches was 0. They engaged with the campaign 30 minutes later, so this send counts as successful.</li><li>On January 3rd at 09:00, they received a second message. They had already received 1 message in the past 7 days (the one on Jan 1), so prior_touches = 1. They engaged again — this time 3 hours later. Another successful send.</li><li>On January 5th at 10:00, they received a third message. They had already been contacted twice in the past 7 days, so prior_touches = 2. This time, they did not engage at all, so this send is marked as failed.</li></ul><p>User 2: </p><ul><li>On January 1st at 09:00, they received their first message that week. <code>prior_touches</code> = 0. They engaged with the message an hour later. Successful send.</li><li>On January 2nd at 10:00, they received a second message. They had received 1 message in the past 7 days, so prior_touches = 1. This time, they did not engage within 24 hours. Failed send.</li><li>On January 4th at 11:00, they received a third message. They now had 2 messages in the past 7 days (Jan 1 and Jan 2), so prior_touches = 2. Again, they did not engage. Another failed send.</li></ul><p>GLHF! </p><p>P.S. This kata is part of the a hands-on SQL kata series focused on real-world campaign analytics, user engagement behavior, and event-based systems. All tasks revolve around two central tables: <code>campaign_sends</code> and <code>engagement_events</code>.</p>
